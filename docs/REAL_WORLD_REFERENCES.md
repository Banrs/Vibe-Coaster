# Real-World References — Consolidated Audit

## 1. Purpose

This document is the single, cross-referenced ledger of every project constant that is derived
from (or intentionally deviates from) a real-world roller-coaster record, geometry figure,
physics coefficient, or industry-standard practice. It exists so that any datum in the generator
can be **retraced by an external auditor** back to: the exact value in code, the real-world basis
for that value, the primary source(s) for the real-world figure, the "law" (project rule) that was
applied to turn the real figure into the in-repo constant, and an honest status flag — including
flags for uncited, unverifiable, or internally-conflicting figures. Nothing here launders
uncertainty: where a source audit could not confirm a figure, that is recorded as
`UNVERIFIED_SOURCE` or `NOT_FOUND_IN_CODE`, not silently upgraded to a confirmed record.

This file is a **merge of six prior audit documents** (per-domain research passes, each performed
2026-07-20 against the working tree at `/home/user/Vibe-Coaster`), consolidated here verbatim in
substance — every citation, verdict, and flag from the source audits is preserved. It does not
re-verify their findings; it organizes them for traceability.

**Audit date: 2026-07-20.** All "current record" / "current" language throughout this document is
as of that date; several cited records are explicitly noted as superseded or defunct as of that
date, and each such row says so.

### 1.1 The two project laws under audit

1. **GEOMETRY LAW (dimensional references — heights, radii, drops, lengths, speeds).** The
   reference constant for a geometric element must equal the **real-world RECORD** for that
   element (the most extreme *built* example, cited to a source) — not a "typical" ride. At build
   time, the generator scales that reference into a window of **[1.0x, 1.5x]**
   (`genc::RECORD_SCALE_MIN` / `genc::RECORD_SCALE_CAP`, `v1/gen_constants.h`; **UPDATE
   2026-07-21**: user raised the floor from 0.75x), and the **built population mean must land
   near ~1.25x** (the window midpoint).  Draw centring is **SPEED-AWARE** (user law 2026-07-21):
   the entry-speed percentile maps linearly onto the scale-window position (lower-quartile speed
   -> ~1.125x, upper-quartile -> ~1.375x; `frndUp`, `v1/coaster_track.cpp`), which simultaneously
   enforces the **DURATION LAW**: built mean element duration ~0.75x of `REAL_ELEMENT_SECONDS`
   (`REAL_DURATION_BIAS 0.65-0.85`, centre 0.75) with no element AVERAGING >1.0x real.
   **RATIO LAW (project-wide)**: every real-coaster parameter enters as a DIMENSIONLESS RATIO of
   that coaster's own quantity applied to OUR quantity under the project scalings — never an
   absolute-value transplant.
2. **G-FORCE LAW (per-element felt-g targets).** Per-element felt-g targets = **2x the real-world
   RECORD** for that element type (not 2x a typical/average coaster — records can already run far
   hotter than a normal ride, and the extreme end is the correct anchor). User anchor example:
   Intimidator 305's banked turn sustains ~4 g real -> our banked turns should target ~8 g. All
   g-force targets are additionally hard-gated by the repo's measured **vertical per-sample
   envelope of [-6.5, +12] g** (`v1/coaster_track.cpp:1493` reject in `spatialForceClear`;
   reported/flagged `src/main.cpp:699,711,463`), a **lateral cap of |lat| <= 6.0 g**
   (`genc::LATERAL_G_ENVELOPE`, gated `src/main.cpp:808`), and a **sustained-mean acceptance
   floor** of mean positive >= +9.4 g AND mean negative <= -4.5 g on intense lobes
   (`src/main.cpp:872`, printed target "+10/-5g band +9.4/-4.5" at `:856`) — this floor can reject
   a ride that's too tame but can never clip a high target.

Both laws are project-internal design rules, not physical necessities; they are recorded here as
the yardstick against which every value below is judged (`OK` = law satisfied, `TOO_HIGH`/
`TOO_LOW` = law violated in a stated direction, `INTENTIONAL_DEVIATION` = law knowingly not
applied for a documented reason).

## 2. How to read the tables

Every row uses this column set: **Parameter | Our constant (file:symbol, value) | Real-world
basis (value, coaster, park, year) | Source (name + URL) | Law applied | Status/flags**.

Status/flag vocabulary:
- `OK` — constant satisfies the applicable law within tolerance.
- `TOO_HIGH` / `TOO_LOW` — constant is materially outside the law's target band.
- `UNCITED` — no source of any kind exists in-code or externally for this figure.
- `UNVERIFIED_SOURCE` — a source is claimed (in-code comment or research pass) but could not be
  independently corroborated with a public citation.
- `NOT_FOUND_IN_CODE` — the codebase has no reference constant/citation for an element the law
  says should have one.
- `INTENTIONAL_DEVIATION` — a documented, deliberate departure from the law (e.g. a hardcoded
  1.0x floor instead of 0.75x, explained in-code).
- `PENDING-FIX` — the source audit recommended a specific new value; that value is given.
- `PROJECT_CHOICE` — not a record-derived figure at all; a project engineering/design decision
  (ASTM-derived clearance, water-fraction directive, lap-duration directive). Recorded for
  traceability, not judged against the record laws.

**Envelope-clip analysis (from `gforce_2xwr_audit.md`):** the **+12 g hard cap binds the loop
family**. 2x of the modern loop record (Shock Wave 5.9 g) = 11.8 g (just under the cap); 2x of a
plain 6 g circular loop = 12 g **exactly at the cap**; 2x of the historic extreme (Moonsault 6.5 g
-> 13 g; Flip Flap 6-8 g -> 12-16 g) **exceeds +12 g**. The loop's current `gT=10` sits below the
cap today, but raising it to a true 2x-record target (12 g) would sit exactly on the cap with zero
interpolation margin — and 12 g sustained already exceeds human blackout tolerance, so this is
flagged as a place where the 2x-record law and physical realism collide; it needs a user decision
rather than a silent raise. The **-6.5 g floor has headroom** (2x of the -2.2 g ejector record is
only -4.4 g); it does not clip any current 2x-airtime target. The **+9.4/-4.5 g sustained band is
a floor, not a clip**, and cannot cap a high target.

  distinct from an operating-cycle duration;
- equal-weight primary-pair active-run midpoint: **132.5 s**;
- supply normalization: **per 60 active seconds**, growing continuously with
  longer acts;
- Tormenta inventory: exactly **two Immelmanns, one loop, one cutback**.

The 4-slot sustained-inversion budget is a comfort ceiling, not a target.
`LOOP`, `CUTBACK`, and `IMMEL` each receive a `0.50/min` soft supply.
Comparator `ROLL` is a late no-cutback CLASSIC fallback at `0% / 0.0 per min`; its former supply has
been transferred intact to the distinct cutback, keeping the controlled
partition at exactly 100%. Whole airtime-family traversal time is reported separately from true
sub-1g airtime, so a 22-26% element-family time reading is not described as
22-26 seconds of ejector/floater exposure.

The live `--forceaudit` additionally reports three rider-frame thresholds:
below +1 g (broad unloading), below +0.25 g (the conventional floater-airtime
boundary used by Ride Forces Database), and below 0 g (weightless/negative).
On the final eight-seed calibration, the intentional HILLS/BANKAIR/FLOATSTALL

132.5 s run. Those measured values, rather than the 42.4% count share or 22.8%
